The Amazon seller supply chain problems nobody talks about publicly

You're generating real sales on Amazon. But behind the numbers, the logistics setup is quietly eating your profit — and it gets worse every time Amazon changes its rules.

  • 💸

    You're paying Amazon to store inventory that hasn't sold yet

    You sent 6 months of stock in a single container because the per-unit freight cost was lower. Smart at the time. But now Amazon is billing you $2.40 per cubic foot per month in Q4, your IPI score is under pressure, and half that stock is still sitting in a fulfilment centre.   • 🏭

    You've added a US 3PL to work around Amazon's storage limits — and now you're paying twice

    Receiving fees. Monthly storage. Pick and pack per pallet. Then another freight bill to move it from the 3PL into Amazon. At some point you stopped doing the maths because the answer was too uncomfortable. There has to be a better way.

  • 📦

    You're coordinating 3, 5, or more suppliers in China with no central hub

    Factory A ships this week. Factory B ships next week. Factory C is still "almost ready." You're chasing separate tracking numbers, forwarding Amazon labels to multiple contacts, and hoping nobody screws up the prep.   • 🏷️

    You've had an FBA prep rejection — and dealt with it remotely at full cost

    Wrong FNSKU label. Missing poly bag. Barcode covered by packaging. Amazon rejects the shipment or charges you a non-compliance fee. Fixing a prep error from 8,000 miles away is expensive, slow, and humiliating. And it usually happens when you're already out of stock on the listing.

FAQ

Questions Amazon sellers ask before switching

How is this different from just using a freight forwarder in China?
A freight forwarder's job ends at the port. Ours begins there. We receive goods from multiple suppliers, consolidate them, QC inspect, FBA prep (labels, poly bags, carton labels), handle all customs documentation, and automatically upload tracking numbers to your Seller Central account. A freight forwarder hands you a container. We hand you stock that's already in Amazon's system.
Will drip-feed shipping actually improve my IPI score?
In most cases, yes — significantly. IPI is affected by your excess inventory ratio and sell-through rate. When you maintain only 4–8 weeks of cover in Amazon instead of 5–6 months, your sell-through improves and your excess inventory drops. Many sellers see their IPI rise 20–40 points within a quarter of switching to drip-feed. We can walk you through the maths for your specific situation.
What if one of my weekly shipments is delayed or lost?
This is exactly why drip-feed protects you better than bulk shipping. When you send weekly shipments, a single delay means your next shipment arrives 7 days later — not 3 months later. Additionally, all shipments through us are fully insured. If goods are lost or damaged in transit, we manage the claim and reimburse you. You're never one container away from a ranking-destroying stockout.
Can you handle FBA prep for products with complex requirements?
Yes. We regularly handle poly bagging for apparel and soft goods, bubble wrap for fragile items, suffocation warning labels, set and bundle creation, over-labelling, expiry date labelling, and custom carton dimensions for non-standard products. If your product has specific requirements, share the spec and we'll confirm feasibility before you commit.
What does the 30-day free storage offer actually include?
When your first goods arrive at our Shenzhen facility, your 30-day free storage period begins and runs for exactly 30 days from that date. There are no storage charges during this period, no matter your volume. After 30 days, the standard rate of $0.49 per CBM per day applies. No credit card required to start, no hidden fees, no automatic contract commitments.
Do you integrate with Amazon Seller Central directly?
Yes — full integration. Your product SKUs sync to our platform. When you create a shipment, it creates the corresponding shipping plan in Seller Central automatically. Carton labels are printed in our Shenzhen warehouse. Tracking numbers are uploaded to Seller Central the moment your shipment is dispatched. You manage the whole operation through a single dashboard without touching Seller Central's complex shipping workflow.
