How It All Connects

What the Old Model Quietly Costs You

The old eCommerce fulfillment model is slow, yes. But the real problem is structural cost you can't see on any single invoice.

Cash flow is the obvious problem. But the traditional bulk-shipping model extracts cost at every stage — most of it invisible until you add it up.

  • 💸

    You're paying a US 3PL to hold inventory that never needed to leave China

    The entire purpose of your overseas 3PL is to store goods closer to your customers. But if your customers are global — or if delivery speed from China is competitive — you're paying rent on a warehouse you don't need, in a country you don't sell enough into to justify it.

  • 📈

    Dead stock doesn't just cost storage — it blocks capital from your next winner

    Overordering one SKU means underordering the next. The capital locked in slow-moving stock is capital you can't deploy into marketing, product development, or the product that's actually selling. Smaller, more frequent orders reduce this structural problem permanently.

  • 🚚

    Shipping a container is the most expensive way to test a new market

    Launching into a new country with container volumes means huge upfront commitment to an unproven market. Direct fulfilment from China lets you test any market with minimal inventory — and scale only the ones that perform.

  • 🙋

    Your brand experience ends where your fulfilment partner takes over

    If your 3PL doesn't support custom packaging, branded inserts, or personalised packing — your unboxing experience is generic. We pack in your branded materials at the standard $0.99 per order rate. No surcharges. No compromise.

  • ⏳

    12-hour time zone gaps make every issue a multi-day problem

    Your supply chain is in China. Your 3PL is in the US. Your freight forwarder is in a third time zone. Every coordination email loses a business day. When your fulfilment partner is in the same city as your suppliers, problems get resolved in hours — not a week of relay emails.

Frequently Asked

Questions D2C founders ask before switching models

How long does delivery from China to the US or UK actually take?
Using our express air freight routes, delivery to the US typically takes 5–8 business days from dispatch. UK delivery is typically 5–7 business days. Economy tracked services are available for price-sensitive routes. Tracking numbers are generated at dispatch and automatically uploaded to your Shopify store.
Will my customers know the package was shipped from China?
Not if you choose otherwise. We partner with carriers who inject packages directly into domestic delivery networks — in the US this typically means USPS last-mile delivery with a US-format tracking number, so the experience is indistinguishable from a domestic shipment. Combined with your branded packaging, the origin is not visible to your customer.
Can you use my branded packaging for every order?
Yes — and no additional per-order charge for using your branded packaging vs a plain outer. Ship your branded boxes, poly mailers, tissue paper, inserts, or stickers to us once and we apply them to every outbound order as standard. Same $0.99 pick and pack rate either way.
What about customs duties — will my customers get unexpected charges?
DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) shipping is available on major routes including the US and UK, meaning all duties are settled before delivery and your customer receives their package with no surprise charges. We advise on the most duty-efficient carrier and declaration approach for your specific product category.
How does the Shopify integration actually work in practice?
Once onboarded, your Shopify store connects to our fulfilment system. Every order placed flows through automatically — no manual export or email required. We pick, pack, and dispatch. Tracking numbers upload back to Shopify and trigger your standard customer shipping confirmation. Setup typically takes 3–5 business days.
Is there a minimum order volume?
No minimum. We work with brands processing 50 orders per month and brands processing 5,000. New customers receive 30 days of free warehouse storage — no credit card, no commitment. If direct China fulfilment isn't the right fit after testing it, you've lost nothing but a conversation.
What product types work best with this model?
Lightweight to medium products — typically under 1.5–2kg per order — where the air freight cost is proportionate to the product value. Items with an average order value above $25–$30 tend to work best. Top categories: beauty, skincare, accessories, homeware, small electronics accessories, stationery, supplements, and apparel.
Can you handle customer returns?
Yes. We design a returns process around your brand's economics — local returns address that batches back to us, direct-to-China returns for higher-value items, or disposal for low-value returns where shipping cost outweighs recovery value. We agree on the approach during onboarding.
