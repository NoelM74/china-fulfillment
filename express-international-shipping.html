DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) — No Surprise Charges for Your Customers

DDP means all customs duties and import taxes are calculated and paid before the shipment leaves China. Your customer (or your Amazon FC) receives the goods with zero additional charges. We offer DDP on express, air freight, and special line shipments to most countries. This is the recommended option for D2C brands shipping directly to end customers, because unexpected duty charges at delivery are the single biggest cause of refused packages and negative reviews.

Frequently asked questions

How fast is express shipping from China to the US?
DHL express from Shenzhen to the US takes 2-4 business days. FedEx Priority takes 2-4 days. UPS Express takes 3-5 days. These are door-to-door transit times including customs clearance. Add 1 day for warehouse processing if shipping from stored inventory.
How much does express shipping from China cost?
Express shipping via DHL, FedEx, or UPS starts from approximately $8.50 per kilogram to the US. Rates vary by weight, dimensions, destination, and carrier. We negotiate wholesale rates across all clients, so even small shippers get volume pricing. Contact us for exact rates based on your package profile.
What is the difference between DDP and DDU shipping?
DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) means all customs duties and import taxes are paid before the package ships. The recipient pays nothing at delivery. DDU (Delivered Duty Unpaid) means the recipient pays duties and taxes on arrival. DDP is recommended for D2C brands to prevent customer complaints about unexpected charges.
Can you ship sensitive goods like batteries, liquids, or cosmetics?
Yes. We handle sensitive goods including products with built-in batteries, liquids up to 500ml, non-white powders, food products, supplements, cosmetics, branded goods, and PPE or medical supplies. Sensitive cargo routes through Hong Kong with 3 flights per week to major destinations. We handle all required documentation and declarations.
What is a special line and how does it compare to express?
Special lines are regional shipping routes we operate through consolidated cargo arrangements. They deliver in 6-12 days, which is faster than sea freight but slower than express. The cost is 30-40% less than DHL or FedEx express. Special lines are available to the US, UK, Europe, Middle East, Southeast Asia, and Australia.
What is the cheapest way to ship from China?
Sea freight is the cheapest option for bulk cargo, starting from approximately $300 per CBM. For small parcels, postal packets start from $3.20 per kilogram. Special lines offer a middle ground at $5.80 per kilogram with 6-12 day delivery. The best option depends on your shipment weight, urgency, and destination.
Do you handle customs clearance?
Yes. We handle all customs documentation, declarations, and clearance for every shipping method. For DDP shipments, duties and taxes are calculated and paid at origin. For DDU shipments, we prepare all documentation to ensure smooth clearance at the destination. Express shipments via DHL, FedEx, and UPS receive priority customs handling.
Can I combine shipping with warehouse storage and fulfillment?
Yes. Shipping integrates with all our warehouse services. Inventory stored in our Shenzhen warehouse at $0.49 per CBM per day can be shipped via any method: express for urgent D2C orders, air freight for Amazon FBA replenishments, or sea freight for bulk consolidation shipments. Same warehouse, same team, one invoice.
