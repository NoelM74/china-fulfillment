The Real Problem

The freight forwarding headaches every Amazon seller knows by heart

Freight is one of the biggest costs in your supply chain — and one of the most error-prone. Here's where it goes wrong most often.

  • 💰

    Your "competitive" freight rate looks very different by the time you see the final invoice

    Origin charges. Destination handling fees. Fuel surcharges. Peak season surcharges. Documentation fees. Port security fees. The headline rate your freight forwarder quoted was accurate — it just didn't include any of the fees that doubled it. Getting a truly all-in freight rate requires asking the right questions, and most first-time importers don't know what those questions are.

  • 📦

    You're shipping small volumes and paying proportionally huge rates for the privilege

    LCL shipping for 2 or 3 CBM from a standalone forwarder is expensive per unit — you don't have the volume to negotiate rates, and you're filling the gap between bigger shipments. The rate you pay as a small seller bears no resemblance to what large importers pay. This is where consolidation changes everything.

  • 📋

    Coordinating shipping labels between your supplier, freight forwarder, and Amazon is a constant source of errors

    You create a shipping plan in Seller Central. Download the labels. Email them to your supplier. Wait to confirm they printed correctly. Then upload tracking numbers manually after dispatch. Any breakdown in this chain — a wrong label applied, a missed tracking upload — creates a receiving delay or a Seller Central discrepancy that takes weeks to resolve.

  • 🗺️

    You want to expand to new markets but freight complexity is holding you back

    You're profitable on Amazon US. You know there's money on Amazon UK, Amazon DE, Amazon AU. But you don't know how to ship there, who to trust, what documents you need, or how to compare the landed cost in each market. Every time you try to work it out, the complexity sends you back to focusing on what you already know.

  • ⏱️

    A delayed shipment has cost you a ranking before — and the risk never fully goes away

    Bulk shipments are high-stakes bets. Everything in one container. If it's delayed by weather, port congestion, or a customs hold, you run out of stock. Your ranking drops. Your PPC cost jumps to rebuild it. You told yourself it wouldn't happen again — but without a fundamentally different approach to shipping cadence, the same risk is still there every cycle.

These problems all have the same root cause: too many separate parties managing your freight, with no one responsible for the whole picture. We fix that.

FAQ

Freight forwarding questions answered

What's the difference between LCL and FCL shipping — which should I use?
FCL (Full Container Load) means you fill and pay for an entire container — typically a 20ft or 40ft box. LCL (Less-than-Container Load) means your goods share a container with other shippers' goods. For most Amazon sellers sending regular replenishment shipments rather than entire container-loads at once, LCL through our consolidated service gives you far better flexibility and cash flow, without sacrificing rate competitiveness because we consolidate across hundreds of clients.
How does your insurance work if a shipment is lost or damaged?
Every shipment we handle is fully insured from our Shenzhen warehouse door to the Amazon FBA receiving dock (or other destination). If goods are lost or damaged in transit, we manage the insurance claim and reimburse you directly — typically within 14 days of the claim being validated. You don't need to chase the carrier. We've handled claims resulting from carrier strikes, transit damage, and port incidents, and our clients were reimbursed in full each time.
How does tracking work — can I see where individual cartons are?
Yes — carton-level tracking is available through your dashboard. Each carton has a unique tracking reference that updates as it moves through our warehouse, customs clearance, the carrier network, and into Amazon's receiving system. Tracking numbers are automatically uploaded to Seller Central at the time of dispatch, so your Seller Central account is always current without any manual work from you.
Can you ship to multiple Amazon FBA fulfilment centres in one shipment?
Yes. When Amazon's shipping plan splits inventory across multiple fulfilment centres (which it often does for inventory placement), we manage the split shipping accordingly — directing the correct quantities to each destination as specified in the Seller Central plan. This is handled automatically through our Seller Central integration.
How do I get a freight quote?
Contact us with your product type, approximate dimensions and weight per carton, total CBM or carton count, and your destination. We'll provide an all-in quote covering freight, customs, and delivery to Amazon — no hidden surcharges added later. For ongoing replenishment clients, we set up standing rate agreements so you always know your cost per shipment before it moves.
