FAQ

Questions about QC & FBA prep from China

When do you inspect goods — before or after they arrive at your warehouse?
We inspect goods after they arrive at our Shenzhen warehouse, once your supplier has delivered them. This means we're inspecting the exact units that will be shipped to Amazon — not a pre-shipment sample. Every defect is documented and reported to you before anything is moved forward, giving you full control over next steps.
What happens if you find a quality problem?
We flag the issue to you immediately with photographs, a defect count, and a written description. At that point — because goods are still in China — you have three options: (1) request your supplier fix the issue at their cost, (2) have us perform the rework or repackaging in our Shenzhen facility, or (3) reject the batch and return to your supplier. All of this happens before the goods ever leave China.
Can you handle relabelling if my supplier used the wrong labels?
Yes. Relabelling is one of our most common requests. We remove incorrect labels, apply your correct FNSKU labels, verify scannability, and document the change. If the underlying packaging is non-compliant (e.g., Chinese-language only), we can transfer products into new English-language packaging as well.
Do you stay current with Amazon's changing FBA prep requirements?
Yes — we monitor Amazon's FBA prep requirements continuously. Our operations team updates internal prep checklists whenever Amazon changes its requirements, and we adjust our processes accordingly. With 15 years of FBA prep experience, we've navigated every major change Amazon has made to its receiving requirements.
Can you create bundles or multi-pack sets from separate units?
Yes. We regularly create multi-unit sets and product bundles — combining individual units, applying set labels, and ensuring the finished bundle meets Amazon's set creation requirements. Share your bundle specification and we'll confirm feasibility and pricing before you commit.
