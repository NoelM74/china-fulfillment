Every Amazon Seller Knows This Pain

The supplier problems that quietly consume your time and erode your margins

These aren't unusual situations. They're the default experience for sellers managing Chinese suppliers without on-the-ground support.

  • 😴

    Your supplier goes quiet 2 weeks before your shipping deadline — and then claims everything is "almost ready"

    No update. Messages on WeChat read but not replied to. Then, suddenly, "goods are ready to ship" — except they're not quite right, or the quantity is off, or the labels aren't done. You discover this the week your stock is supposed to leave port. Managing timelines from overseas means you always find out too late to respond effectively.

  • 📉

    You ordered 500 units. You received 463. Your supplier disputes it — and you have no proof

    Short shipments are common. Suppliers don't always disclose them, and the invoicing doesn't always reflect it. Without someone physically counting goods on arrival and documenting discrepancies, you often don't discover the shortage until much later — by which point recovering it from the supplier is nearly impossible.

  • 📦

    You have 4 suppliers. All 4 are shipping at different times, to different addresses, with different freight forwarders

    Coordinating multiple suppliers to align on a single shipment timeline is a full-time job. Every supplier has their own idea of "ready." Every freight forwarder quotes differently. You're spending hours each week on logistics coordination that has nothing to do with actually growing your business.

  • 🔄

    Your supplier makes changes to your product without asking — and you find out when your customers do

    Material substitution. Component change. Packaging update. A different colour that's "close enough." Without someone inspecting every batch against your exact approved specification, your supplier has no real accountability for product consistency. And by the time customer reviews tell you something changed, the damage is already done.

  • 💸

    You've paid for rejects you couldn't return — because proving the defect was your supplier's fault is too hard from overseas

    You receive bad stock. You know it's your supplier's problem. But you can't prove it without documentation from the time of production or arrival — and by the time you've photographed enough evidence to make a case, the supplier has a dozen reasons why it's actually your fault. Without on-the-ground inspection, you absorb costs that should never have been yours.

Every one of these situations gets dramatically easier when you have a trusted partner physically present in China, monitoring your suppliers and coordinating your supply chain on your behalf.

FAQ

Questions about China supplier management

How many suppliers can you manage for one client?
There's no hard limit. We work with clients who have 2 suppliers and clients who have 20+. Our warehouse can receive from unlimited suppliers simultaneously, and our coordination team manages communication with each one. The more suppliers you have, the more value our consolidated receiving and single-shipment approach delivers.
Do your suppliers need to be in Shenzhen for this to work?
No. Suppliers can be anywhere in China. Goods from Guangdong, Zhejiang, Jiangsu, and other manufacturing provinces all route to our Shenzhen warehouse via domestic freight — which is typically arranged by your supplier as part of their delivery obligation, often at minimal cost to them (sometimes cheaper than delivering FOB to a nearby port). We coordinate the domestic logistics as part of our service.
How do you communicate with suppliers who only speak Mandarin?
Our Shenzhen-based team is Mandarin-speaking and conducts all supplier communication in Chinese. This is a significant practical advantage — suppliers are more forthcoming with a local partner than with overseas clients, respond faster, and comply more readily with requests for documentation or rework. You receive English-language summaries of all communications.
What happens if a supplier delivers a completely unacceptable batch?
We document the issue immediately with photographs and unit counts, then contact you with our inspection report and a recommended course of action. Options typically include: (1) requesting the supplier collect and replace the goods at their cost, (2) having us perform rework or repackaging in Shenzhen, or (3) formal rejection of the batch. Having photographic evidence from our team at the time of arrival is far more effective than trying to dispute a shipment weeks later.
Is there a minimum volume to use your supplier management service?
No minimum volumes. We work with sellers who are just getting started with 2-3 suppliers and a few hundred units per order, as well as established sellers with complex multi-SKU operations. Contact us with your current supplier setup and we'll design a service arrangement that fits your stage of business.
